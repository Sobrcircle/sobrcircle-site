a note before you begin

at the time of writing, I just turned thirty six.

but if i'm being honest with myself, and this book is nothing if not that, i'm really turning seventeen.

because somewhere around sixteen, i stopped growing. and it took me twenty years, four pivotal relationships, two trips to rehab, and more relapses than i want to admit, to finally find god and figure it out.

this book is those twenty years.

there are twenty four poems across four chapters. each poem has twenty four stanzas. that's not an accident. there are twenty four hours in a day and i've learned that what you do inside them is everything. it's who you are when no one is watching. it's what you reach for when the silence gets loud. it's whether you choose the same thing again or finally choose something different.

each poem follows the other chronologically. this is my life, in order, from the moment i first picked up to the moment i finally put it all down and walked toward something real.

to the four women who played the most significant role in this. you each shaped a chapter of who i became. i felt hurt by all of you at different times. but writing this made me see something i wasn't ready to see before. i was the problem too. this book is not an accusation. it's an apology. it takes two, and i own my half.

and to whoever is reading this.

maybe not every page will be yours. but i hope one of them is. i hope somewhere in these twenty four stories you find the part that sounds like your life and realize you're not alone in it.

that's all i ever wanted this to be.

not a book about addiction. a book about twenty years of trying to figure out how to be a person and finally finding the path.

and through him, i found freedom.

maybe you will too.

bm

i want to acknowledge my daughters. i haven't always been the father i needed to be. i'm still working on that. but i love them more than this book could ever say, and everything i'm becoming is for them.

to my three daughters.

you are too young to read this now. but one day you might. and when you do, i want you to know that your father wrote every word of it trying to become someone worthy of you.

there are moments in these pages where i didn't want to be here anymore. and every time, it was the thought of you that brought me back. not because life got easier. but because leaving would have meant you carrying that pain. and i couldn't do that to you.

before each of you came into this world, you were already saving my life. you just didn't know it yet.

you three have always been my reason. every single time.

i love you more than this book could ever say.
